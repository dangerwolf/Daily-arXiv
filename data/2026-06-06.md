### [10] [IIKL: Isometric Immersion Kernel Learning with Riemannian Manifold for Geometric Preservation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.06288)
*Zihao Chen,Wenyong Wang,Jiachen Yang,Yu Xiang*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 本研究提出了一种名为等距浸入核学习（IIKL）的新方法，用于在非欧几里得数据中保留几何和拓扑结构，通过将数据映射到黎曼流形并保持内积不变，从而提高了数据重建和分类等下游任务的准确性。

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有的非欧几里得数据表示学习方法通常将数据映射到欧几里得空间，可能丢失关键的几何信息。本研究旨在解决这一问题，提出一种能够保留原始数据内在几何结构的新方法。

Method: 提出等距浸入核学习（IIKL）方法，构建黎曼流形并从中诱导黎曼度量。证明了等距浸入等价于流形切丛上的核函数，保证了学习过程中任意切空间中向量内积的不变性。基于IIKL提出了一种参数化学习模型，并使用最大似然估计（MLE）推导出交替训练方法。

Result: 实验结果表明，所学黎曼流形及其度量成功地保留了3D和高维数据集的内在几何表示，并将下游任务（如数据重建和分类）的准确性显著提高。与现有最先进（SOTA）方法相比，内积不变量损失减少了90%以上，下游重建准确性平均提高了40%，涉及等距和共形的几何指标误差降低了90%。

Conclusion: IIKL方法能够有效地在非欧几里得数据中学习其内在的几何表示，通过保持内积不变来保留数据的几何结构，并在多种下游任务中取得了显著的性能提升，优于现有SOTA方法。

Abstract: Geometric representation learning in preserving the intrinsic geometric and topological properties for discrete non-Euclidean data is crucial in scientific applications. Previous research generally mapped non-Euclidean discrete data into Euclidean space during representation learning, which may lead to the loss of some critical geometric information. In this paper, we propose a novel Isometric Immersion Kernel Learning (IIKL) method to build Riemannian manifold and isometrically induce Riemannian metric from discrete non-Euclidean data. We prove that Isometric immersion is equivalent to the kernel function in the tangent bundle on the manifold, which explicitly guarantees the invariance of the inner product between vectors in the arbitrary tangent space throughout the learning process, thus maintaining the geometric structure of the original data. Moreover, a novel parameterized learning model based on IIKL is introduced, and an alternating training method for this model is derived using Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE), ensuring efficient convergence. Experimental results proved that using the learned Riemannian manifold and its metric, our model preserved the intrinsic geometric representation of data in both 3D and high-dimensional datasets successfully, and significantly improved the accuracy of downstream tasks, such as data reconstruction and classification. It is showed that our method could reduce the inner product invariant loss by more than 90% compared to state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods, also achieved an average 40% improvement in downstream reconstruction accuracy and a 90% reduction in error for geometric metrics involving isometric and conformal.

### [12] [RelServe: Fast LLM Inference Serving on Relational Data](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.11546)
*Xin Zhang,Shihong Gao,Yanyan Shen,Haoyang Li,Lei Chen*

Main category: cs.DB

TL;DR: relQuery服务面临延迟瓶颈，RelServe通过动态优先级更新和自适应批次安排解决了HoL阻塞问题，将平均服务延迟降低了3.1倍。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 随着AI驱动的电子表格等应用程序广泛采用relQuery服务，在并发查询负载下实现快速响应时间变得越来越重要，但当前的LLM引擎面临严重的延迟瓶颈。

Method: RelServe引擎包含两个核心创新：1.动态优先级更新器（Dynamic Priority Updater），通过统计近似不断调整优先级并最小化开销；2.自适应批次安排器（Adaptive Batch Arranger），定量评估候选预填充和解码批次，以最小化预测平均延迟。

Result: 在四种真实世界数据集和13B到70B参数的LLM上进行的大量实验表明，RelServe将平均服务延迟降低了高达3.1倍，优于vLLM。

Conclusion: RelServe是一种优化的LLM引擎，可实现低延迟的relQuery服务。

Abstract: The use of Large Language Models (LLMs) for querying relational data has given rise to relQuery, a workload pattern that applies templated LLM calls to structured tables. As relQuery services become more widely adopted in applications such as AI-powered spreadsheets, fast response times under concurrent query loads are increasingly important. Unfortunately, current LLM engines face severe latency bottlenecks from Head-of-Line (HoL) blocking across three comparable inference phases: waiting, core running, and tail running. Existing static priority scheduling methods only address HoL blocking during the waiting phase, leaving two critical problems unsolved. First, the absence of a priority update mechanism causes inaccurate prioritization and continued HoL blocking during core execution. Second, suboptimal prefill-decode batching exacerbates HoL blocking in tail execution and worsens latency trade-offs between running and waiting relQueries. To address these problems, we propose RelServe, an optimized LLM engine for low-latency relQuery serving. RelServe features two core innovations: a Dynamic Priority Updater that continuously adjusts priorities while minimizing overhead via statistical approximations, and an Adaptive Batch Arranger that quantitatively evaluates candidate prefill and decode batches to minimize projected average latency. Extensive experiments on four real-world datasets using LLMs ranging from 13B to 70B parameters show that RelServe reduces average serving latency by up to 3.1x compared to vLLM.
